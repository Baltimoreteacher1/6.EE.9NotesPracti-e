📖 What Are Independent & Dependent Variables?

Standard 6.EE.9 – Learn the key vocabulary before you start solving!

🎮

Independent Variable

The variable YOU control or change.
It is the input.
It lives on the x-axis.
It does NOT depend on anything.

🎯

Dependent Variable

The variable that changes because of the independent variable.
It is the output.
It lives on the y-axis.
It DEPENDS on the input.

💡 Memory trick: The dependent variable is like a pet — it depends on its owner (the independent variable) to survive!

🌍 Real-World Examples

🚗
Road Trip
Independent (x): hours driven
Dependent (y): miles traveled
Miles depend on how long you drive.
🍕
Pizza Party
Independent (x): number of pizzas
Dependent (y): total cost
Cost depends on how many pizzas you order.
🌱
Plant Growth
Independent (x): days of sunlight
Dependent (y): height of plant
Height depends on sunlight received.
💰
Babysitting
Independent (x): hours worked
Dependent (y): money earned
Earnings depend on hours worked.
📚
Reading Challenge
Independent (x): number of books read
Dependent (y): points earned
Points depend on books read.
🎵
Music Downloads
Independent (x): songs downloaded
Dependent (y): total data used
Data used depends on songs downloaded.
🔑 Key Questions to Ask:
✅ "Which variable CAUSES a change?" → Independent
✅ "Which variable IS CHANGED by the other?" → Dependent
✅ "Which comes first / is the input?" → Independent

=15×
🎮 Scenario 2: A gamer earns 50 points for every level completed. The player decides how many levels (L) to finish. The total points (p) earned depends on levels completed.

=50×
🥤 Scenario 3: A smoothie needs 3 fruits per serving. You pick how many servings (s) to make. The total fruits needed (f) depends on how many servings you make.

Build: dependent = 3 × independent

=3×
🔑 Pattern: Always written as dependent = (rate) × independent → y = kx. The dependent variable is always isolated on the left side!
